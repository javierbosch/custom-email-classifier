Health and wellbeing: Self-help resources

SilverCloud offers mental health treatment programmes based around Cognitive Behavioural Therapy. The service has a range of programmes you can access, focusing on mindfulness, sleep, resilience, chronic pain, health anxiety and even Covid-19. Find out more about the range of self-help and online resources available to our University community via our website <https://mailings.ed.ac.uk/27JU-1LFUH-9FSYIA-11MHFA-1/c.aspx> .


Exams and assessment

We know how challenging these new ways of studying and assessment are for many of you. Staff involved in marking and assessments are working hard to ensure that you are in no way disadvantaged by the impact of the pandemic, through changes to procedures such as Special Circumstances. Find out more <https://mailings.ed.ac.uk/27JU-1LFUH-9FSYIA-11MHFB-1/c.aspx> .

Be aware of scams and cyber attacks

We have been made aware of a number of scams targeted towards students in higher education. Some of these threats centre on scammers calling or emailing students and asking for bank details. If you have been the victim of telephone or online fraud, contact Police Scotland on 101. Find out how to protect yourself from cyber threats via our Information Security webpages <https://mailings.ed.ac.uk/27JU-1LFUH-9FSYIA-11MHFC-1/c.aspx> .

EdHelp: new support hub coming soon

The University will soon launch a new 'EdHelp' service to make it easier for students to find the resources they need. The hub will launch in June and will provide clear and straightforward access to key services, including Student Administration, Student Finance, Library and IT, all in one place. The Careers Service have now launched Students as Change Agents as an online programme and are welcoming students to take part digitally.
